## 1. What is Aegis?

**Aegis is a women's safety app that can silently send SOS alerts, share your live location, and collect evidence — without you needing to visibly use your phone.**

Unlike a normal safety app, Aegis is disguised as a **Calculator**. To anyone who sees your phone, it looks like a calculator. Only you know how to unlock the real safety system.

### Why the disguise matters

In dangerous situations, an attacker may see your screen. Opening an app called "Emergency SOS" could make things worse. Aegis solves this — it stays hidden unless you specifically unlock it.

### 3 ways to trigger SOS

| Method | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Manual SOS** | You can access your phone safely |
| **Voice SOS** | Phone is in your pocket / you can't look at it |
| **Motion SOS** | Sudden physical struggle or phone snatch |

## 4. Setting Your Secret PIN

The first time you use Aegis, you must create a secret PIN. This PIN is how you unlock the real safety app from the calculator screen.

### First-time setup

1. Open the Calculator
2. Type a PIN of your choice (for example: `2580`)
3. Press `=`
4. The app will ask you to confirm this PIN
5. Enter it again to confirm

Your PIN is now set. **Remember it — this is the only way to open Aegis.**

---

### Unlocking Aegis in the future

Every time you want to open the real Aegis app:

1. Open the Calculator
2. Type your secret PIN
3. Press `=`

The safety dashboard will open.

> Anyone who types random numbers will only see calculator results.
> Only you know the PIN.

---

### Important PIN Rules

- **Do not share your PIN** with anyone you do not fully trust
- **Do not use an obvious PIN** like `1234` or `0000`
- **Choose something memorable** but not guessable
- If you forget your PIN, you will need to reinstall the app

After logging in, Aegis will request access to several device features. You **must allow all of them**.

---

When asked:

### Microphone — Allow Microphone Access

When asked:
- Allow microphone access

### Camera — Allow Camera Access

**Why it's needed:** Used to record video evidence during SOS.

When asked:
- Allow camera access

### Notifications — Allow Notifications

**Why it's needed:** Aegis uses notifications for status updates and SOS confirmation.

When asked:
- Allow notifications

## 7. Adding Emergency Contacts

> **You must add contacts before Aegis can send any SOS alerts.**

Emergency contacts are the people who will receive your SOS messages, live location, and evidence links.

### How to add a contact

1. Open the Aegis home screen (after unlocking with your PIN)
2. Find the **Emergency Contacts** section
3. Tap **Add Contact**
4. Fill in:
   - **Name** (e.g., "Mum")
   - **Phone Number** (with country code, e.g., +91 98765 43210)
   - **Relation** (e.g., Mother, Friend)
5. Tap **Save**

Repeat for each contact you want to add.

### Who should you add?

- A parent or sibling
- A close trusted friend
- A partner or guardian
- Anyone who can take immediate action if they receive an alert

> Add at least **2–3 contacts**. If one phone is off or unreachable, others will still be alerted.

## 8. How SOS Works

Once SOS is triggered, Aegis runs automatically in three phases:

### Phase 1 — Immediate Alert
- Sends an SOS SMS to all your emergency contacts
- Includes your current GPS location as a Google Maps link

### Phase 2 — Evidence Collection
- Starts recording 30 seconds of audio and video
- Continues sending live location updates

### Phase 3 — Evidence Delivery
- Uploads the audio and video to secure cloud storage
- Sends one final SMS to all contacts with the evidence links

All three phases happen automatically, without you needing to do anything further.

## 9. The 3 SOS Trigger Methods

### 1. Manual SOS (Fastest & Highest Priority)

**Best when:** You can access your phone and press the button directly.

**How to use:**
1. Unlock Aegis with your PIN
2. Tap the **SOS Button** on the home screen
3. SOS starts immediately

Manual SOS:
- Activates instantly
- Bypasses cooldown timers
- Has the highest priority in the system

---

### 2. Voice SOS (Hands-Free)

**Best when:** Your phone is in your pocket, someone is watching you, or you cannot touch your phone.

**How it works:** Aegis listens in the background and detects emergency words.

**Trigger phrases (examples):**
- "Help"
- "Help me"
- "Save me"
- "Bachao"
- "No"
- "Leave me"

When Aegis hears one of these:
1. A short alarm sounds
2. A countdown begins
3. If not cancelled, SOS activates automatically

**Languages supported:** English, Hindi, Hinglish

> You can add or remove trigger phrases in the Safe Words settings.

---

### 3. Motion SOS (Auto-Trigger)

**Best when:** There is sudden physical struggle, phone snatch, or forceful movement.

**How it works:** Aegis monitors your phone's accelerometer. If it detects sudden, violent movement consistent with a struggle or a fall, it starts the pre-SOS countdown.

Useful in situations like:
- Phone grabbed from your hand
- Sudden physical assault
- A fall or collision

---

## 10. The Pre-SOS Alarm & How to Cancel

For **Voice** and **Motion** triggers (automated), Aegis does not immediately send SOS.

Instead:
1. **An alarm sounds** — loud, to alert people nearby
2. **A countdown starts** — you have a few seconds
3. **If not cancelled, SOS activates**

This prevents false alarms from accidental keyword detection or normal movement.

### How to cancel

If the trigger was accidental:

- Tap the **Cancel** button before the countdown ends

The alarm will stop. No SOS will be sent. Aegis returns to its normal listening state.

> **Manual SOS does not have a pre-SOS delay.** It activates immediately.

---

## 11. Safe Words

Safe words are phrases you can speak to cancel an automated SOS countdown.

**Default examples:**
- "I am safe"
- "I'm safe"

You can add your own safe word in the app settings.

### When to use safe words

If Voice SOS detected a false trigger, saying your safe word (during the countdown) will cancel the sequence — the same as tapping Cancel.

> Safe words only work during the pre-SOS countdown phase, not after SOS has fully activated.

## 12. Important Android Settings

These settings significantly affect how reliably Aegis works in the background. They are **highly recommended** for all users.

---

### Disable Battery Optimization for Aegis

Android may put Aegis to sleep to save battery, which can prevent voice and motion detection from working.

**Steps:**
1. Go to **Settings → Apps → Calculator**
2. Tap **Battery**
3. Set to **Unrestricted** or **Don't optimize**

---

### Enable AutoStart (Xiaomi, Oppo, Vivo users)

These brands have extra restrictions on apps running in the background. You must manually allow Aegis to start in the background.

**Steps:**
1. Go to **Settings → Apps → Calculator**
2. Find **AutoStart** or **Auto Launch**
3. Turn it **On**

---

### Allow Background Activity

1. Go to **Settings → Apps → Calculator**
2. Find **Background activity** or **Background data**
3. Enable it

---

### Keep Location Always On

For voice and motion SOS to include your location:

1. Go to **Settings → Location**
2. Make sure location is set to **On**
3. Go to **Settings → Apps → Calculator → Permissions → Location**
4. Set to **Allow all the time**

## 14. Troubleshooting

### SOS message was not received

Check the following:
- SMS permission is granted (**Settings → Apps → Calculator → Permissions → SMS**)
- At least one emergency contact is saved with the correct phone number and country code
- Your SIM card is active and has signal

---

### Voice SOS is not activating

Check the following:
- Microphone permission is granted
- Battery optimization is disabled for Aegis
- Background activity is allowed
- You are pronouncing a supported trigger phrase clearly

---

### Motion SOS triggered accidentally

This can happen with vigorous exercise or sudden drops.

Options:
- Increase the motion sensitivity threshold in settings (if available)
- Cancel using the pre-SOS countdown when you know it is a false alarm
- Add a safe word that you can quickly say to cancel

---

### No location in the SOS message

Check the following:
- Location permission is set to **Allow all the time** (not "only while using")
- GPS is turned on in your phone settings
- You are not in a location with very poor GPS signal (indoors, basement)

---

### App is closing in the background

This is usually caused by battery optimization. Follow the steps in [Section 12](#12-important-android-settings) to fix this.

If you are on Xiaomi, Oppo, or Vivo, make sure AutoStart is enabled.

---

### App asks for PIN after reinstall

If you reinstall Aegis, your PIN is reset. You will need to set a new PIN on first launch. Emergency contacts are also cleared and must be re-added.

## 15. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can someone else unlock the app if they see me using it?**
A: Only if they know your PIN. To anyone else, it looks like a calculator. They would need to know your specific PIN and press `=` to unlock it.

---

**Q: Does Aegis work without the internet?**
A: Yes, for the most critical part — the initial SOS alert with your location is sent via SMS through your SIM card directly. Evidence upload and backend sync require internet, but your contacts will still be alerted even if you're offline.

---

**Q: What happens if I accidentally trigger SOS?**
A: For voice and motion triggers, you have a short countdown to cancel. Tap **Cancel** or say your safe word before the countdown ends. Manual SOS does not have a cancel window — use it only when you intend to.

---

**Q: How many emergency contacts can I add?**
A: You can add multiple contacts. All of them will receive the SOS SMS, location, and evidence links simultaneously.

---

**Q: Does Aegis record audio or video when I'm not triggering SOS?**
A: No. The microphone is only used to detect trigger words during the voice listening phase. Full audio and video recording only begins after SOS is confirmed.

---

**Q: Will my contacts know my PIN?**
A: No. The SOS messages sent to contacts contain your location and evidence links only — not your PIN or any other private settings.

---

**Q: What if my phone is dead?**
A: Aegis cannot function without battery. Keep your battery charged, especially in situations where you may need protection.

---

**Q: Does the app work on iPhone?**
A: Currently, Aegis is Android-only. iOS support is being evaluated for a future version.

---
